## 📚 Từ Vựng — Data Storytelling

| Cụm | Nghĩa | Ví Dụ |
|---|---|---|
| **the story these numbers tell** | câu chuyện từ số liệu | The story these numbers tell is one of steady improvement. |
| **behind the numbers** | bên dưới các con số | But behind the numbers, there's a real human story. |
| **what this means in practice** | điều này có nghĩa thực tế | What this means in practice is fewer late nights. |
| **to put that in context** | để hiểu ngữ cảnh | To put that in context: last year, we had 3 production outages. |
| **translate into** | chuyển thành | That 15% improvement translates into 2 days saved per sprint. |
| **the real cost is** | chi phí thực sự là | The real cost isn't the bug — it's the trust we lose. |
| **paint a picture** | dựng lên bức tranh tổng thể | Let me paint a picture of where we were 6 months ago. |

## 🗣️ Mẫu Câu — Story + Data Structure

```
HOOK: "Six months ago, our pass rate was 60%."
CONTEXT: "To put that in context — we were shipping with known bugs 
          because we simply didn't have time to fix them all."
DATA: "Today, we're at 85% — a 25-point improvement."
MEANING: "But the real story isn't the number. 
          It's that in the last 2 sprints, 
          we had zero Severity-1 production bugs. Zero."
IMPACT: "That translates to roughly 40 hours of hotfix work 
         we didn't have to do — and a lot of user trust preserved."
FUTURE: "So the question is: how do we sustain this?"
```

## 🎧 Shadowing Script — Data Story

```
"Let me paint a picture of where we were a year ago.

QC was the bottleneck. Every release was delayed 
because bugs were found too late. 
Developer-QC relationships were strained.
And honestly? The team's morale was low.

Now look at these numbers.
Average defect escape rate: down 60%.
Time to release: down from 3 weeks to 18 days.
Production incidents in Q4: zero.

But the story behind the numbers is the real win.
The dev team now loops QC in from sprint planning.
Testers are writing test cases alongside requirements.
We've built a culture where quality is everyone's job.

That shift didn't happen because of a tool or a process.
It happened because the team trusted each other.
And that took time, and honest conversations."
```

## 💬 Speaking Prompt (Ghi Âm 3 Phút)

> **Tell a 3-minute data story about your QC team's journey this year. Start with a challenge, show the data, and end with the human story behind the numbers.**
