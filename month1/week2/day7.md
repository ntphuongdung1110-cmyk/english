## 📚 Từ Vựng Hôm Nay — Storytelling Language

| Từ / Cụm | Phiên âm | Nghĩa | Ví Dụ |
|---|---|---|---|
| **it all started when** | /ɪt ɔːl ˈstɑːrtɪd wen/ | mọi chuyện bắt đầu khi | It all started when we got a critical bug report at 11pm. |
| **at the time** | /æt ðə taɪm/ | vào lúc đó | At the time, I had no idea how serious it was. |
| **looking back** | /ˈlʊkɪŋ bæk/ | nhìn lại | Looking back, I should have escalated sooner. |
| **turning point** | /ˈtɜːrnɪŋ pɔɪnt/ | bước ngoặt | That was a real turning point for how we do QC. |
| **long story short** | /lɒŋ ˈstɔːri ʃɔːrt/ | nói ngắn gọn | Long story short, we fixed it and shipped on time. |
| **out of nowhere** | /aʊt əv ˈnoʊwer/ | đột ngột, không báo trước | Out of nowhere, the server went down 2 hours before release. |
| **in hindsight** | /ɪn ˈhaɪndsaɪt/ | nhìn lại (sau khi biết kết quả) | In hindsight, we should have done more regression testing. |
| **take [someone] by surprise** | /teɪk baɪ sərˈpraɪz/ | làm ai đó bất ngờ | The bug took everyone by surprise. |
| **ended up** | /ˈendɪd ʌp/ | cuối cùng (thực tế đã) | We ended up delaying the release by a day. |
| **at that point** | /æt ðæt pɔɪnt/ | vào thời điểm đó | At that point, I knew we had a serious problem. |

## 🗣️ Mẫu Câu Hôm Nay — Storytelling Structures

### Pattern 1: Mở đầu câu chuyện
```
"So this happened about [time] ago when I was [context]."
→ So this happened about a year ago when I was managing my first big release.

"I remember this one time when [situation]."
→ I remember this one time when we caught a critical bug just 2 hours before go-live.

"It all started when [event]."
→ It all started when the product team decided to add a new payment method last minute.
```

### Pattern 2: Xây dựng tình huống
```
"At the time, [description of situation]."
"The challenge was [what made it hard]."
"What made it worse was [additional complication]."
→ At the time, half my team was on leave. The challenge was we only had 6 hours to fix it.
   What made it worse was the dev lead was unreachable.
```

### Pattern 3: Mô tả hành động
```
"So what I did was [action]. And then [next action]."
"I decided to [action] because [reason]."
→ So what I did was pull together whoever was available and we split the testing areas.
   I decided to personally handle the critical payment flows because that was the highest risk.
```

### Pattern 4: Kết thúc và bài học
```
"In the end, [outcome]. Looking back, [reflection]."
"Long story short, [short conclusion]."
"What I took away from that experience was [lesson]."
→ In the end, we caught 3 critical bugs and delayed 12 hours. Looking back, that 12-hour 
   delay saved us from a potential customer data issue.
→ What I took away from that experience was: always have a contingency test plan.
```

## 🎧 Shadowing Script — Work Story

```
"So there was this one time that really stuck with me.
We were about to release a major update — 
it had been in development for like 3 months — 
and the night before go-live, out of nowhere, 
one of my testers finds this bug.

And at first I'm thinking, okay, minor issue, 
we can document it and release anyway. 
But then she shows me the impact — 
it was affecting user data. Like, actual user data.

At that point, I had a decision to make. 
Do we delay? Do we release with a known risk?

I called the PM. I laid out the facts. 
I said, look — this is Severity 1, it affects 
roughly 30% of users, and we cannot ship this.

Long story short, we pushed the release by one day. 
And honestly? It was the right call. 
Looking back, I'm glad we caught it when we did."
```

- Không cần câu chuyện hoàn hảo — cứ kể tự nhiên
