## 🗣️ Mẫu Câu Hôm Nay

### Pattern 1: Nói về điểm mạnh hiện tại
```
"One of my strengths is [skill/quality]. 
This has helped me [concrete example]."
→ One of my strengths is attention to detail. 
   This has helped me catch critical bugs that others might overlook.
```

### Pattern 2: Nói về areas for improvement (interview style)
```
"One area I'm actively working on is [skill].
I've been [specific action] to address this."
→ One area I'm actively working on is public speaking. 
   I've been volunteering to present in team meetings more often to build that confidence.
```

### Pattern 3: Career goals
```
"In the short term, my goal is to [1–2 year goal].
Longer term, I see myself [5-year vision]."
→ In the short term, my goal is to build out a strong automation framework for our team.
   Longer term, I see myself taking on a broader quality leadership role — 
   potentially across multiple product lines.
```

### Pattern 4: What drives you
```
"What motivates me most is [driver].
I find [activity] particularly rewarding because [reason]."
→ What motivates me most is seeing quality improve over time — measurably.
   I find coaching junior testers particularly rewarding because 
   I can see the direct impact on the team's capability.
```

## 🎧 Shadowing Script — Career Talk

```
"Someone asked me recently where I see myself in 5 years, 
and honestly, I had to think about it.

Right now, I'm really focused on deepening my expertise 
in QA strategy and test automation — 
because I think that's where the industry is going.

But in terms of career trajectory, 
I see myself eventually moving into a 
broader quality leadership role — 
maybe Head of QA or VP of Engineering Quality. 
Somewhere where I can influence quality culture 
at an organizational level.

What drives me is this: 
I genuinely believe that great software 
starts with great quality thinking. 
And I want to be part of building that 
in whatever organization I'm in.

In terms of areas for improvement — 
I'm actively working on my executive communication skills. 
Like, being able to present quality data 
in a way that resonates with non-technical stakeholders. 
That's something I'm intentional about right now."
```
